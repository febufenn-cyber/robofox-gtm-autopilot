# Solo-Founder GTM

Protect the founder’s scarce time, cash, attention, and trust. AI may increase throughput, but the founder retains customer conversations, judgment, relationships, pricing approval, and high-stakes sends.

## Required context

Read the relevant product file and establish:

- Stage and paying-customer count.
- ACV and required sales assistance.
- Monthly cash ceiling.
- Existing audience and local network.
- Founder hours available for selling and delivery.
- Channels already available and failed experiments.
- Biggest constraint: distribution, proof, product, time, or cash.

## Founder versus agent ownership

| Agent may assist | Founder owns |
|---|---|
| Research and account briefs | ICP and market choice |
| First drafts | Final voice and send approval |
| CRM summaries | Customer conversations |
| Data normalization | Pricing and scope |
| Weekly analysis | Keep/iterate/kill decisions |
| Competitive monitoring | Partnerships and trust |

Never let automation conceal the absence of real customer contact.

## Stage rules

### Pre-first-10-customers

Primary objective: find a narrow segment that pays and succeeds.

- Prioritize owner conversations, demonstrations, proposals, and delivery learning.
- Use manual workflows to discover what must eventually be automated.
- Sell a bounded result, not an imagined platform.
- Collect objections and proof systematically in HubSpot.
- Avoid broad paid acquisition, heavy automation, and complex tooling.

### Early repeatability

After several paid wins in a coherent segment:

- Document the repeatable trigger, buyer, scope, onboarding, and outcome.
- Test two or three channels with pre-registered criteria.
- Automate research/reporting before automating relationships.
- Raise or restructure pricing when delivery economics are understood.

### Scale

Only after a channel and offer are repeatable:

- Increase throughput in the proven channel.
- Add agents for repetitive research, drafting, CRM hygiene, and analytics.
- Hire or contract when founder bottlenecks cause missed revenue or delivery risk.

## Motion decision table

| IF | THEN |
|---|---|
| ACV below $1K and value is immediate/self-serve | Product-led may work. |
| ACV $1K–5K or setup requires guidance | Sales-assisted founder motion. |
| ACV above $5K, workflow change, or multiple stakeholders | Founder-led consultative sale. |
| Implementation is local/high-trust | Use local network, walk-ins, referrals, and founder demos. |

For Robofox voice agents, default to founder-led sales-assist until evidence proves self-serve onboarding.

## Weekly founder operating system

Allocate time deliberately:

- Customer discovery and sales conversations.
- Delivery and proof collection.
- Follow-up and CRM hygiene.
- One owned distribution asset.
- Weekly evidence review.

Batch research and drafting. Keep high-context conversations live and personal.

## Tooling principles

- Prefer existing HubSpot and Meta MCP access over duplicate dashboards.
- Cap the active GTM tool stack.
- Stay monthly before a tool proves essential.
- Do not buy enterprise plans for speculative future needs.
- Do not build a UI for internal analysis when Markdown plans/reviews work.
- Track source, conversation, demo, proposal, deal, and outcome consistently.

## AI-agent deployment order

1. Research summaries.
2. Drafting and repurposing.
3. CRM/data quality checks.
4. Weekly analytics and anomaly detection.
5. Scheduling assistance.
6. Only then consider higher-volume outreach operations—and still require human send review.

## Channel discipline

Use `channel-portfolio-selector` to rank at most five channels. Every channel needs:

- One owner.
- One primary KPI tied to owner conversations or demos.
- A weekly operating cadence.
- A pre-registered kill criterion.
- A defined founder-time budget.

A channel is not working merely because content or features were shipped.

## When to hire

Consider a first GTM hire or contractor only when:

- A proven channel has more qualified work than the founder can process.
- Follow-up delays are losing real deals.
- The role has a documented process and measurable output.
- Gross margin and cash runway can support the commitment.
- Hiring will multiply a proven motion rather than search for one.

Use fractional or project help before permanent headcount when the bottleneck is temporary.

## Deliverables

A solo-founder GTM response should include:

1. Current stage diagnosis.
2. The founder’s single commercial objective.
3. What the founder owns versus delegates.
4. Weekly time allocation.
5. Minimal tool/agent stack.
6. Channel and evidence priorities.
7. Explicit activities to stop or defer.
8. Next review date and decision rule.

## Robofox guardrails

- Monthly budget below $500 means owned channels until first revenue.
- Founder-led/local channels outrank paid acquisition.
- Every outbound message is a draft until reviewed.
- No skill may change an ad, send a message, or accept pricing automatically.
- HubSpot-derived matching metrics override external benchmarks after at least 30 relevant data points.

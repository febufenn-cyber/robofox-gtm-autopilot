## Ranked portfolio

### 1. Founder-led local sales: introductions, walk-ins, and live demos

This is the closest route to clinic and coaching-centre owners in Madurai and nearby Tier-2 markets. The offer requires trust, workflow understanding, setup, and sales assistance, so direct founder conversations fit the ACV and stage better than broad reach. Use local introductions, permission-based walk-ins, and tightly scoped demos to learn missed-call pain, language needs, current receptionist workflow, and willingness to pay. The channel’s job is not scale yet; it is to produce qualified owner conversations, demos, and the first proof-backed customer.

- **Primary KPI:** Qualified owner conversations and demos booked.
- **Kill criterion:** Zero real owner conversations after three weeks of genuine effort → iterate the target segment or opening once, then kill the current segment/channel version if it still produces zero conversations.

### 2. WhatsApp-first warm referral and follow-up funnel

WhatsApp matches the available funnel and the local buying environment, but it must remain permission-based and relationship-led. Ask every genuine contact, demo participant, and local connector for an introduction; then use WhatsApp to share a short founder-reviewed explanation, book a demo, and follow up. Do not scrape numbers or blast templates. This channel ranks second because it converts existing local trust into conversations without paid acquisition and creates a clean handoff into HubSpot.

- **Primary KPI:** Owner conversations and demos booked from consented WhatsApp introductions.
- **Kill criterion:** Zero real conversations after three weeks of genuine, consented referral effort → iterate the referral ask and proof asset once, then kill this funnel version if it still produces zero conversations. Pause immediately if consent, opt-out, or DND handling is inadequate.

### 3. Light signal-based outbound, human-reviewed only

The $1K–15K ACV rule permits light outbound, but the benchmark file shows weak average cold-email economics and a higher spam-flag risk for AI-written messages: 3.43% average replies, top decile ≥10.7%, and ~8% spam-flagging for AI-written versus ~3% human-written, all with vendor bias. Use narrow signals—such as a clinic/coaching centre visibly relying on calls or advertising admissions—verify every recipient, draft one-to-one messages, and require founder review before any send. This is a learning channel, not an autonomous SDR system.

- **Primary KPI:** Positive owner replies, owner conversations, and demos booked.
- **Kill criterion:** Reply rate below 2% after 500 verified sends and two copy iterations → kill. Alert and pause if complaint rate exceeds 0.1% or bounce rate exceeds 2%. Never auto-send.

### 4. Bottom-funnel GEO/entity and proof pages

Generic informational SEO is not selected: the benchmark reference reports an approximately 58% position-1 CTR reduction under AI Overviews and explicitly favors comparison/entity pages over generic content. Build only pages that help a buyer evaluate the offer—for example, what the receptionist handles, Tamil/English workflows, clinic/coaching-centre use cases, pricing logic, implementation boundaries, and honest comparisons with a missed-call/manual-reception process. Each page should lead to a founder conversation or demo, and every performance claim must remain unverified until customer evidence exists.

- **Primary KPI:** Qualified owner conversations and demos attributed to proof/comparison/entity pages.
- **Kill criterion:** At Day 90, if the page set has produced zero qualified owner conversations after two message/CTA iterations, kill the page-production program. **[PRE-REGISTERED INTERNAL HYPOTHESIS — UNVERIFIED]** Kill generic informational content immediately; retain only proof/comparison/entity work.

### 5. Relevant community and build-in-public conversations

Use founder-led participation in relevant India SaaS, local business, clinic, coaching-centre, and SaaSBoomi-adjacent communities to find connectors, objections, and referral paths—not to accumulate followers. Share concrete lessons from building Tamil+English call handling, ask for workflow feedback, and invite qualified owners into a conversation. Because the existing audience is minimal, this is a support channel beneath direct local selling, not the primary growth engine.

- **Primary KPI:** Qualified owner introductions, owner conversations, and demos booked.
- **Kill criterion:** Zero real conversations after three weeks of genuine contribution → iterate the community or contribution format once, then kill the current community/channel version if it still produces zero conversations.

## First 90 days — weekly milestones

| Week | Milestone | Evidence captured |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | Define one clinic ICP and one coaching-centre ICP; write a problem interview guide and HubSpot source fields. | Approved ICP hypotheses and CRM attribution fields. |
| 2 | Conduct the first founder-led local owner conversations; record missed-call, language, workflow, and buying objections. | Conversation notes and objection tags. |
| 3 | Run permission-based walk-ins/introductions and book live demos; review whether either ICP shows stronger urgency. | Owner conversations and demos booked by segment. |
| 4 | Iterate the weaker local opening once; create one short WhatsApp proof/explanation asset with explicit consent and opt-out wording. | Versioned local pitch and WhatsApp draft. |
| 5 | Ask warm contacts and prior conversations for specific introductions; route every consented lead into HubSpot. | Introduction count, owner conversations, demos. |
| 6 | Review local and WhatsApp channels against the three-week zero-conversation rule; keep, iterate, or kill the current version. | First pre-registered channel verdicts. |
| 7 | Build a verified, narrow signal list for one segment; draft—not send—the first human-reviewed outreach copy iteration. | Verified prospect set and approved draft. |
| 8 | Execute only founder-approved outreach; monitor verified sends, replies, bounces, complaints, conversations, and demos. | Clean denominator and safety metrics. |
| 9 | Publish the first bottom-funnel proof/entity page without unverified performance claims; connect attribution to HubSpot. | Live page and source tracking. |
| 10 | Publish one additional comparison/use-case page and contribute one concrete build lesson in a relevant community. | Page attribution and community conversations. |
| 11 | Run the second outbound copy iteration only if the first has enough evidence; continue founder review on every message. | Reply and conversation data by copy version. |
| 12 | Run the weekly kill review across every live channel using 7-day and 30-day data; stop any conclusively killed version. | `KEEP / ITERATE / KILL / INSUFFICIENT DATA` record. |
| 13 | Consolidate 90-day learning: winning segment, owner language, demos, deal movement, channel verdicts, and first-party sample sizes. | Updated context, next-quarter plan inputs, and benchmark overrides when `n >= 30`. |

## Deferred or excluded channels

- **Meta CTWA / Meta paid acquisition:** Deferred. The `<$500/month` gate requires owned channels until first revenue, and India-first paid activity also requires organic proof. The Meta benchmark warns that new-customer CAC roughly doubled from $257 to $528 in the cited study and that in-platform ROAS should not be trusted alone.
- **LinkedIn ads:** Excluded. The budget gate disallows paid channels, and vendor benchmarks of roughly $3.94 median CPC and ~$8 for SaaS make it a poor current fit.
- **Paid niche newsletters:** Excluded until the budget gate is cleared; despite cited CPC of $1–3, it remains paid placement.
- **Product Hunt/Hacker News launch:** Not selected because the applicable rule requires ACV below $1K plus self-serve; this product is sales-assist at ~$1,200–2,500 ACV.
- **Field sales team / ABM:** Not selected because the product is solo-founder, pre-first-10-customers, and below the >$50K ACV rule.
